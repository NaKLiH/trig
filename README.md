# Trigonometry & The Unit Circle

An interactive visualiser for 14-year-olds exploring sin, cos, and tan through the unit circle.

**Live site: https://naklih.github.io/trig/**

## What it covers

- Why we use a circle — right triangles only go up to 90°, the circle extends that to 360°
- Sin, cos, and tan as fixed ratios (like π) — the same for any triangle with the same angles, no matter the size
- The unit circle — why the hypotenuse is always 1 (it's the radius), and how that lets you read sin and cos straight off the diagram
- Tan as the length along the tangent line to the circle — and why the line is fixed at x=1
- Live sin and cos waves as the angle changes
- The fixed-ratio proof — concentric circles showing that the ratio can't change because the tip must reach the circle
